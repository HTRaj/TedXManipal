OUR SPEAKERS

If your actions inspire others to dream more, learn more, do more and become more, you are a leader.

Aaron Friedland

Modal Header

Mr. Aaron Friedland, is an economics lecturer who also doubles up as a social entrepreneur. The founder and executive director of The Walking School Bus - an initiative aimed at spreading eduction in developing countries, Mr. Friedland always strives to be the wind under the wings of young dreams that will help them to take off.

Gayatri Prabhu

Modal Header

Recipient of the RK Narayan award for her work in the literary field, author of three novels and the memoir 'If I Had To Tell It Again', Gayatri Prabhu has a doctorate in English from the University of Nebraska and currently teaches Literary Studies at Manipal Centre for Humanities. She has championed the cause of mental health among the youth by initiating the Student Support Centre at Manipal.

Srikaanth Viswanathan

Modal Header

Mr. Srikaanth Viswanathan, a 47 year-old man driven by his dreams set a world record when he successfully completed a challenging 14 hour swim across the English Channel. Having battled cramps, jellyfish and the chilling cold weather he became the oldest in the country to achieve this Herculean feat!

Megha Bhatia

Modal Header

A lawyer by profession and a social activist by heart, Ms. Megha Bhatia is adamant towards positive change as she works towards the prevention of child sexual abuse with her brain child - Our Voix, a youth led organization that aims to prevent child sexual abuse by encouraging awareness from a primary stage.

Chhaya Dabas

Ankita Modi

Modal Header

A student, a researcher and a creator, Ms. Ankita Modi, a student from MIT has developed ideas to form research cultures for Nosocomial infections in the hope to help people gauge the gravity of what are commonly known as hospital-acquired infections, and to increase the awareness of this seriously overlooked phenomenon.
